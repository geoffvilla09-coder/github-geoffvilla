Authentic Menudo Recipe

Menudo is a traditional Mexican stew made with tripe and hominy, often served with lime, onions, and cilantro. This recipe serves about 8 people and takes around 3-4 hours to prepare.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs beef tripe (cleaned and cut into bite-sized pieces)
  • 1 lb beef feet or knuckles (optional, for richness)
  • 1 large onion, quartered
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tbsp dried oregano
  • 1 tbsp ground cumin
  • 1 can (15 oz) hominy, drained and rinsed
  • 8 cups beef broth or water
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2-3 dried guajillo chiles (optional, for spice)
  • Fresh lime wedges, chopped onions, and cilantro for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, add the tripe, beef feet (if using), onion, garlic, bay leaves, oregano, cumin, and enough broth to cover. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 2-3 hours until the tripe is tender. Skim off any foam that rises to the surface.
  2. If using chiles, toast them in a dry pan, then soak in hot water for 10 minutes. Blend with a bit of broth and add to the pot for extra flavor.
  3. Add the hominy and simmer for another 30-45 minutes. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Serve hot, garnished with lime, onions, and cilantro. Traditionally enjoyed with warm tortillas.

Note: Menudo is often made the day before serving, as the flavors deepen overnight. Adjust spice levels to taste.
